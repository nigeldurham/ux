blueberry

sign up redesign

our team

onboarded by blueberryx's founder, i directed redesigns for their membership sign up flow. working remotely with blueberryx's engineers, i redesigned their existing user sign up flow

product ideation

with the goal of increasing user sign ups, i analyzed blueberry's existing flow for touch points along the user journey. user engagement was driven with more meaningful content and simplified ctas

problem space

users traversed a complex journey through the originial product - i sought to simplify the route from landing page to sign up, reducing screen number while highlighting meaningful content and interactions

wireframing solutions

simplifying the existing landing page, we wireframed a solution facilitating smoother flow from tile-to-tile, implementing visual assets and simplifying copy

product prototyping

minimizing the amount of visual, copy, and journey-based clutter, we reduced the overall flow complexity to direct users to their end goal with simplicity and ease

usability testing

users were tested in-person or online via video chat while screen sharing. unfamiliar users noted that they felt the design did little to entice them to sign up for a membership

brand-familiar users, however, noted that design was simple, easy to navigate, and fit with the expected product experience and brand aesthetic

feature prioritization

providing succinct, organized information about product incentives proved to be sufficient to engage new users across groups. expanding on product features or functionality did not necessarily improve the user experience

final prototype

a/b testing indicated that these design changes were correlated with significantly increased membership sign ups for the BlueberryX product

our product

a simplified sign up flow leading users seamlessly through BlueberryX's online brand experience
