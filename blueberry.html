sign up redesign

our team

onboarded by blueberryx's founder, i directed redesigns for their membership sign up flow. working remotely with blueberryx's engineers, we dynamically modified existing architecture for the online user experience

product ideation

with the goal of increasing user sign ups, i analyzed blueberry's existing sign up flow for pain points along their user journey. verifying and tackling existing pain points proved that user engagement in-platform was driven by more meaningful content and simplified ctas

problem space

users traversed a winding route through the originial product - i sought to simplify the users' journey, reducing page traversals by consolidating product information and highlighting meaningful content that aligned with user interactions

wireframing solutions

simplifying the existing landing page, we wireframed a solution facilitating smoother flow from tile-to-tile. incorporating new visual assets and simplifying existing copy, users' gaze was directed to ctas that facilitate movement in their user journey

product prototyping

minimizing the amount of visual, copy, and journey-based clutter, we reduced the overall flow complexity to direct users to their end goal with simplicity and ease

usability testing

users were tested in-person or online. brand-unfamiliar users noted that they felt the original design had done little to bring them to sign up for a membership, commenting that assets did not meaningfully highlight product features or answer basic questions about product usage or function

our updated design was seen as highlighting important key features, making the product easier to understand and learn more about

brand-familiar users, however, noted that they felt both designs were in line with the expected product experience

feature prioritization

providing more succinct, organized information about product incentives was engaging for new users. adding highly detailed specifications on product functionality and features did not increase membership sign ups in new users

final prototype

a/b testing showed that our design changes increased sign ups for BlueberryX's product, most notably for brand-unfamiliar users. we saw an 87% increase in sign ups overall, more than half of those represented by brand-unfamiliar users

our product

leading blueberryx's sign ups by a significant margin, our simplified sign up flow greatly increased brand awarenss in the online space, specifically engaging new users with little to no experience with the brand

this engagement brought broader platform gains including expanded opportunities for research and new product feedback
