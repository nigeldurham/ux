aiming to connect user-made events with user-hosted event spaces, we designed an in-app platform that melded seamlessly within the airbnb experience

design goals

we sought to overlap two user paths in the same platform. deploying the canonical ux design process, we explored novel designs for an in-app feature connecting users to one another, threading two user flows seamlessly within the product experience

process

working alongside 4 other designers, i directed the development of the design system and product branding for this experience. we shared all roles within this design process including wireframing, iterative user testing, prototyping, and stakeholder presentations

user journey

bringing together our two personas, a "space-seller" and an "event-thrower," we sought to design a platform where both parties could browse and search for available events or spaces seamlessly

research

user research and contextual inquiry indicated this would be an attractive product for both native and platform-naïve users - beginning with user expectations, we developed a prototype driven by intuitive, mvp-led design and incorporating ux best practices

prototyping

in low-fidelity prototypes, venue owners are able to create a profile for their venue, include relevant venue information, and share openly on the platform. event hosts are similarly able to profile their event, include event details, and share publicly on platform. both parties are able to reach out to one another directly

usability testing

usability testing identified key user pain points. these included issues with the general card layouts, specifically confusion about CTA placement or content, and the use of language that was uncommon or unhelpful

priortizing features

data from user testing and user interviews, in conjunction with stakeholder consideratons, helped us to funnel our core features into a cohesive product solution. addressing our users' primary pain points, we focused on core issues for our final prototype

hi-fi prototyping

functional user experience running on invision. layouts and language were optimized according to best practices, as directed by user feedback. usability testing showed that with this prototype, most users felt the flow was indistinguishable from the existing airbnb experience
