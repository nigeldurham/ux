COVR  case study

bringing traveling musicians &
event spaces together

Ideation

deciding to focus on a niche market unique to new york city, i devised an application for freelance musicians who perform intimate events in bars or small venues

Wireframing

centering-in on a mobile-first, location-based platform, i sought to connect artists with nearby performance spaces : artists are able to create a profile, search for nearby event spaces, and book open performance slots on an extended calendar

Prototyping

running low-fidelity prototypes on marvel, i tested user flows for both businesses and artists. businesses check artist profiles and confirm bookings, while artists create a profile, share media, and message businesses in real time

Usability Testing

businesses noted that a multi-platform approach would be ideal for day-to-day booking, including tablet and mobile options. artists noted concerns about booking flexibility and venue-specific options for performers

Feature Prioritization

messaging and media interactions were prioritized for venue-matching, while artist-led requests were scaled back and social sharing was leveraged for on-the-fly events

Final Prototype

a cross-platform solution allowing artists and venues to confirm bookings and performance details, along with payment information and scheduling

Product

a highly-interactive location-based cross-platform booking calendar and payment system for artists and performance venues
