COVR

case study+

bringing traveling musicians and
event spaces together

Ideation

Focusing on a niche market unique to New York City, I devised an application for freelance musicians performing intimate events in bars or small venues.

Wireframing

Landing on a mobile-first, location-based platform, I sought to connect artists with nearby performance spaces : artists are able to make a profile, search for nearby event spaces, and book open performance slots on an extended calendar. Running low-fidelity prototypes on marvel, I tested user flows for both businesses and artists.

Prototyping

Businesses check artist profiles and confirm bookings, while artists create a profile, share media, and message businesses in real time.

Usability Testing

Businesses noted that a multi-platform approach would be ideal for day-to-day booking, including tablet and mobile options. Artists noted concerns about booking flexibility and venue-specific options for performers.

Feature Prioritization

Messaging and media interactions were prioritized for venue-matching, while artist-led requests were scaled back and social sharing was leveraged for on-the-fly events.

Final Prototype

A cross-platform solution allowing artists and venues to confirm bookings and performance details, along with payment information and scheduling.

Product

A highly-interactive location-based cross-platform booking calendar and payment system for artists and performance venues.
